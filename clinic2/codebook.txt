id
    Unique identifier for each house (integer between 0 and 100000).

zipcode
    Zip code indicating the area where the house is located.
    Possible values: 3520, 3525, 3800.
    This may correlate with build year and other neighborhood characteristics.

lot_len
    Length of the land plot in meters.
    Range: 5.0 – 100.0.

lot_width
    Width of the land plot in meters.
    Range: 5.0 – 100.0.

lot_area
    Total area of the land plot in square meters.
    Can be computed as: lot_len × lot_width.

house_area
    Living area of the house in square meters.
    Around 30 m² indicates a very small house, while 200 m² or more indicates a large house.

garden_size
    Size of the garden in square meters.

balcony
    Number of balconies.
    Common values: 0, 1, or 3.

x_coor
    X-coordinate of the house location.
    Integer between 2000 and 3000.

y_coor
    Y-coordinate of the house location.
    Integer between 5000 and 6000.

buildyear
    Year the house was built.
    Oldest houses date back to around 1100, but most were built in the 20th and 21st century.

bathrooms
    Number of bathrooms in the house.
    Most houses have 1; some have 2 or 3.

taxvalue
    Government-estimated conservative house value (in euros).
    Range: 50,000 – 1,000,000.
    Rounded to the nearest 100 euros.
    Typically slightly lower than the retail value.

retailvalue
    Market value of the house (in euros).
    Range: 50,000 – 1,000,000.
    Rounded to the nearest 1,000 euros.

energy_eff
    Energy efficiency indicator:
        1 = energy efficient
        0 = not energy efficient
    Used for climate policy goals in Utrecht.

monument
    Indicator of whether the house has monumental (historical/architectural) value.
    Such houses are often older and architecturally unique.
    This variable is difficult to assess manually and is sometimes predicted from other features.
